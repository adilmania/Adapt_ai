Strategic management and Strategic planning

We are using the term Strategic managementin the academic field, but it’s synonym ofStrategic planning that is used in the business world. If we want to go deeper, we can distinguish both:

-      Strategic management: refer to the formulation, implementation and evaluation of a strategy. It’s an expression to include every step of the process, from choosing the strategy by measuring its effectiveness. It will also guide the application of the strategy inside the company.

-      Strategic planning: only refer to the strategic formulation; it’s about choosing the strategy that will guide the company.

Vision and Mission

The vision of the company is what the company aspire to be. It describes what the organization would like to be or to achieve on a medium or long-term future. The vision is used in marketing in a certain way to sell products or services of the company, it will serve the communication of the company so customers will be aware of the supposed strategy of the company. This is just an image or a desire to reach a certain point, it’s the main difference with the missions of the company. Missions are more like steps the company have to climb before achieving the vision, we can use missions a a part of the strategy because they’re the reflect of the present. Those missions are focussing on the market, customers, positioning etc.
