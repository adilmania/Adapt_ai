History of PESTLE Analysis

The term PEST has been used in marketing and business over the last 20 years and its true history is difficult to establish. The earliest known reference for “Scanning the Business Environment” appears to be made by Francis J. Aguilarin 1967 who discusses about the “ETPS”, which is a mnemonic for the four sectors that impact his business’ environment: Economic, Technical, Political, and Social. Some time after its publication, Arnold Brownreorganized it as “STEP” for Strategic Trend Evaluation Process, as a way to organise the results of his environmental scanning. Thereafter, this “macro external environment analysis” was modified again to become the “STEPE” analysis (Social, Technical, Economic, Political, and Ecological). During the 1980s, several other authors (such as Fahey, Narayanan, Morrison, Renfro, Boucher, Meccaand Porter) included variations in the orders of the letters: PEST, PESTLE, STEEPLE etc. But there is no order or priority in any of the formats. Now the term PESTLEis well-know worldwide and is popular on Human Resources and in introduction to marketing courses. The framework still knows modifications and alterations, some of the most famous professor in Marketing have already added certain complementary words like an E for Ethics. It adds the element of Demographics while making research on the market.

How to use the PESTLE Framework?

The PESTLE Framework is designed as a business environmental scan. The goal is to get as much information as possible that may influence a business, it’s important to be aware of every factor to establish the strategy planning. It gives a bird’s eye view of the whole environment from different angles. The final idea is to provide safety to whom want to follow an idea, a plan or a project.

All the aspects of this technique are crucial for any business. It’s more than just understanding the market; this framework is one key feature to understand strategic management. It’s not only defining what a company should do, but also help to set up goals regarding the overall strategy. It’s crucial to understand that each factors may have different meaning to different kinds of industries or specialities. But it’s still imperative to any company who wants to develop something new (product, service, technic, etc.). They have to conduct the PESTLE analysiswhich go deeper in different fields than theSWOT analysis.

It may be hard to understand and make the difference between each letters of the PESTLE Framework. It’s a mnemonic for six words, each factor is explained below:

  • Political: This factor determine how a government influence the economy and a specific industry or business. It’s about taxes, fiscal policy, tariff trade and duties according to the type of activity or the revenue generated.
  • Economic: This factor is determinant in an economy that impacts directly a company and may have long term effects on the politics and strategies of the company. The inflation rate, interest rate, currency, etc that can affect the whole activity of a company.
  • Social: This factor is about the social environment of the market, and look at cultural trends, demographics, population analytics etc. Way of buying or demand are not the same according to your location, and you can feel this fact if you export your own products worldwide.
  • Technological: This factor permits to find technologies that may affect the operations of the industry on the market. This refers to, research and development.
  • Legal: This factor has external and internal sides. Certain laws affect the business environment differently in certain. The legal analysis helps for choosing strategies according to the legislation (consumer laws, safety standards, labour laws etc.).
  • Environmental: This factor is crucial and determinant in the PESTLE Framework in particular for certain for tourism, farming, agriculture industry. Factors include climate, weather, geographical location, global changes in climate, environmental offsets etc.
