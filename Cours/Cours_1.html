I. Introduction

To explain and define what is the Strategic Management, we can take the example of the race to the South Pole leads by two competitors: Roald Amundsen and Robert Falcon Scott. Both tried to be the fastest to reach the “Southest” point on Earth. They had two different strategies and we will use this story to explain how a strategy choice can affect totally the result of an operation.

In the table below, we can see various strategic choice made by both adventurers regarding the expedition. All those choices result in the winning of Amundsen and the death of Scott (and his whole team).

Amundsen

Scott

-       Prepare everything in advance.

-       Plan risks

-       Focus on one goal: reaching the South Pole

-       Concentrate on one goal and made modification to be faster.

-       Plan the trip with professionals of extreme weathers and live with them for a month.

-       Walked the same distance everyday

-    Set different goals: reaching the South Pole, winning the race and studying nature for scientific purposes.

-         No risks taken, he used known roads.

-       No time management, didn’t use all the time he had.

-       Large team.

-       Used mass-produced equipment.

-       At the end, Scott was to proud to listen to Amundsen’s advises.

-       Walked a lot then it was easy or didn’t walk when it was to hard

Now we can apply those strategic decisions on the real market. The idea here is to to know which is the best: growing slowly by focusing on the simple objective of reaching the final goal or trying to set up multiple objectives by growing fast when the market allows it but trying to minimise loss when the market become harder.

Amundsen’s technics

Scott’s technics

-       Not sure about the environment, neither the market.

-       Use a regular system, step by step with the same goal of 20%.

-       Use self-control to keep in mind the goal, even during good years for the market.

-       This strategy could be applied for long terms strategies.

-       Reach a lot of steps when there are good conditions, but do nothing when there are bad conditions.

-       Spontaneous and follow the vagaries from the market.

-       Use no discipline and grow as fast as they can.

Finally, with Amundsen’s technics the company grow slowly but in good conditions and have enough time to become stable to face troubles on the market. On the other hand, with Scott’s technics, the company may grow fast, but maybe to fast. So it won’t be able to get a stable position and it won’t be possible to adapt to the market modifications. But we can admit that the Amundsen technic may frighten investors for being to slow and comforting. The technic coming from Scott can attract investors who want to see their capital growing quickly when the market is good.

We can say that strategic choice in a company can’t be taken without thinking, it’s part of a long process that will guide the whole company in a short, medium and long-term. Every part of the company is affected by the strategy; all department will align their own strategy to serve the overall strategy. That’s why it’s crucial to set up reachable goals and to learn how it’s important to stay modest while setting up the strategy. Everything is a matter of dosing and measuring to adapt the company to the market, the opposite is not possible.
